2025-26 NBA Rookies

Victor Wembanyama is a French professional basketball player widely regarded as one of the most exciting talents in the world. Born on January 4, 2004 in Le Chesnay, France, he stands about 7 ft 4 in tall and plays as a forward/center. Wembanyama was selected first overall by the San Antonio Spurs in the 2023 NBA Draft and quickly became one of the NBA’s brightest young stars due to his rare combination of size, skill, shooting, and defensive ability. He was named NBA Rookie of the Year and has been selected as an NBA All-Star, leading the league in blocks and making history with record performances. Before the NBA, he rose through French professional basketball, earning MVP honors and multiple accolades in France’s top leagues. Internationally, he has represented the French national team, including at the 2024 Paris Olympics.

Valdez Drexel “V.J.” Edgecombe Jr. is a Bahamian professional basketball player for the Philadelphia 76ers of the NBA. Born on July 30, 2005 in Bimini, The Bahamas, he played college basketball at Baylor University, where he emerged as one of the nation’s top freshmen and earned Big 12 Freshman of the Year honors before declaring for the NBA draft.Edgecombe was selected third overall in the 2025 NBA Draft by the 76ers and quickly made an impact in his rookie season with scoring, playmaking, and defensive contributions. Known for his versatility and athleticism on both ends of the floor, he wears #77 and has become a promising young guard in the league.

Brandon Miller is an American professional basketball player for the Charlotte Hornets of the NBA. Born on September 18, 2005 in Indianapolis, Indiana, he played college basketball at Alabama, where he was a standout freshman and earned SEC Freshman of the Year honors before declaring for the NBA draft. Miller was selected second overall in the 2025 NBA Draft by the Hornets and has quickly become a key contributor with his scoring ability, athleticism, and versatility as a forward. Known for his work ethic and potential, Miller wears #8 and is considered one of the most promising young players in the league.
